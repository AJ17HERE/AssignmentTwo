Kalamkari is a traditional Indian textile art form that originated in the southern state of Andhra Pradesh. The word "kalamkari" is derived from two Persian words: "kalam" meaning pen and "kari" meaning work, and the art form involves using a pen to draw intricate designs on fabric. There are two types of kalamkari: Srikalahasti style and Machilipatnam style. Srikalahasti style involves the use of natural dyes and intricate patterns drawn with a pen made from bamboo or palm leaves. Machilipatnam style involves block printing, where the designs are carved into a wooden block and printed onto the fabric. Kalamkari art is known for its intricate designs, elaborate storytelling, and use of natural dyes made from plants, roots, and minerals. The themes of kalamkari art include Hindu mythology, nature, and folk tales. The art form was traditionally used to decorate temple hangings, cloths, and dress materials. Today, kalamkari art has gained popularity around the world and is used on a variety of products such as sarees, bedspreads, and home furnishings. The art form has also been recognized by the Indian government as a Geographical Indication (GI) product, to protect and promote the traditional art form.
