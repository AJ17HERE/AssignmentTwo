Meenakari is a traditional Indian art form that involves decorating metal objects, such as jewelry, with vibrant and intricate designs. The word "meenakari" comes from the Persian word "meena," which means enamel, and the art form involves coloring and decorating metal surfaces with enamel inlay. Meenakari is believed to have originated in the city of Jaipur in Rajasthan, India, and has been practiced for centuries. The process of meenakari involves etching designs onto the metal surface and filling in the engraved areas with enamel powder, which is then heated to form a glossy and colorful finish. The designs used in meenakari art are often intricate and vibrant, with floral and geometric patterns being common themes. Meenakari art is widely used in the creation of jewelry, including necklaces, earrings, bracelets, and rings. Today, meenakari art has gained popularity around the world and is used on a variety of metal objects such as vases, boxes, and decorative items. The art form has also been recognized as a Geographical Indication (GI) product by the Indian government, which aims to protect and promote the traditional art form.
