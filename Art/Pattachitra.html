Pattachitra is a traditional art form that originated in the eastern Indian state of Odisha. It is a form of cloth-based scroll painting, where intricate designs and narratives are painted on a piece of cloth or palm leaf using natural dyes and pigments. The word "pattachitra" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "Patta," meaning cloth, and "Chitra," meaning picture. The art form is known for its intricate details, bright colors, and use of geometric patterns and motifs. The themes of Pattachitra art are mainly religious, with scenes depicting Hindu deities, epics like Ramayana and Mahabharata, and various legends and myths. Pattachitra artists use a special type of brush called "Rekha Badi" to paint the intricate details and designs. Pattachitra has evolved over the years, and today, it is used on various items such as saris, wall hangings, and other decorative items. The art form has gained recognition globally and is widely appreciated for its vibrant colors, intricate designs, and cultural significance.
